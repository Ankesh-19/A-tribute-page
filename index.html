Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 – 2015

Missile Man of India • The People's President

Early Life

Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Abdul Kalam came from a humble family. His father was a boat owner and imam, and his mother was a homemaker. From a young age, he showed curiosity, discipline, and a deep love for learning.

Education & Career

Kalam studied physics at St. Joseph’s College and later aerospace engineering at MIT, Chennai. He worked at DRDO and ISRO, leading India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III). He played a major role in Agni and Prithvi missile projects, earning the title “Missile Man of India.”

Presidency

In 2002, he became the 11th President of India. Known as the “People’s President,” he inspired millions through simplicity, humility, and his vision for youth empowerment.

Books & Teachings

After serving as President, he dedicated his life to teaching. His books like Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020 continue to motivate young dreamers.

Legacy

Dr. Kalam passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. He lived teaching. He passed away teaching. His legacy continues to guide generations toward innovation, integrity, and service.
